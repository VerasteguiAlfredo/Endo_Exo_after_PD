## TABLES
Table 1 & 2 — already done (baseline + operative/pathologic characteristics)
Table 3 — Incidence and Timing of Endocrine and Exocrine Dysfunction
The core descriptive outcomes table. Reports cumulative incidence of new-onset DM, insulin requirement, exocrine insufficiency, and PERT use, with median time to each event and stratified by resection type if sample size allows.
Table 4 — Risk Factors for New-Onset Diabetes (Multivariable Logistic Regression)
Univariable and multivariable logistic regression for predictors of post-PD DM. Key candidates based on your data: age, BMI, pre-op HbA1c category, gland texture, pancreatic duct diameter, POPF, soft gland, family history of DM, NAT, tumor pathology.
Table 5 — Risk Factors for Exocrine Insufficiency (Multivariable Logistic Regression)
Same structure as Table 4 but for exocrine insufficiency/PERT use. Key candidates: gland texture, pancreatic duct size, pathologic diagnosis, margin status, POPF, drain placement, TPN use.
Table 6 — HbA1c Trajectory by Diabetes Status (optional but strong)
Median HbA1c at each time point (pre-op, Y1–Y10) stratified by whether patients developed DM vs. did not. Shows the divergence over time clearly in tabular form to complement the figure.

## FIGURES
### Figure 1 — Cumulative Incidence of New-Onset DM (Kaplan-Meier or cumulative incidence curve)
Time-to-event curve from surgery to DM diagnosis. This is your primary endpoint figure. If you want to account for competing risk of death, use a cumulative incidence function (CIF) instead of KM — more rigorous for this population given mortality.
### Figure 2 — HbA1c Trajectory Over Time (Spaghetti + Smooth)
Two-panel figure:
- Panel A: Mean/median HbA1c ± IQR at each year (Y1–Y10) for the full cohort
- Panel B: Same but stratified by DM vs. no DM — shows the divergence of glycemic trajectories

This is visually compelling and tells the story of progressive endocrine dysfunction.

### Figure 3 — Cumulative Incidence of Exocrine Insufficiency
Mirror of Figure 1 for the exocrine endpoint. Can be combined with Figure 1 as a two-panel figure to save space and show both primary endpoints side by side.
### Figure 4 — Forest Plot of Predictors of New-Onset DM
Odds ratios with 95% CI from the multivariable model in Table 4. Standard for surgical oncology journals and much more readable than a table alone.
