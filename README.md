# Endo_Exo_after_PD

Pancreatectomy‑Related Endocrine and Exocrine Dysfunction

Study span: 2024-2010

## Study Population
- We conducted a retrospective cohort study of patients who underwent pancreatectomy at Mayo Clinic. Patients were eligible for inclusion if they had no pre-operative diagnosis of diabetes mellitus and no documented use of insulin or oral hypoglycemic agents (including metformin and sulfonylureas) prior to surgery. Eligibility was determined through manual review of the medical record, ensuring that patients with subclinical or pharmacologically managed dysglycemia were excluded even in the absence of a formal diabetes diagnosis. Patients with incomplete surgical or follow-up records were excluded. The final analytic cohort comprised metabolically naive patients at baseline, allowing for the unambiguous attribution of post-operative endocrine and exocrine dysfunction to the sequelae of pancreatectomy.
## Outcomes
- The primary endocrine outcome was new-onset diabetes mellitus following pancreatectomy, consistent with type 3c (pancreatogenic) diabetes. The primary exocrine outcome was pancreatic exocrine insufficiency, defined by clinical diagnosis and/or initiation of pancreatic enzyme replacement therapy (PERT). Time-to-event was calculated from the date of surgery to the date of first documented diagnosis for each outcome respectively. Patients with pre-operative exocrine insufficiency were identified and retained in the cohort but flagged as a covariate given the clinical relevance of pre-existing exocrine dysfunction as a risk factor for post-operative outcomes.
