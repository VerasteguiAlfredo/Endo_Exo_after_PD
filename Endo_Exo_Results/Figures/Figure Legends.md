### (Flowchart caption) Figure X. Study Cohort Assembly and Selection of Patients Undergoing Pancreatectomy.
Flow diagram of cohort derivation. Of 2,000 patients (1995–2024), 1,226 with complete data (2010–2024) were screened. Exclusions included pre-existing diabetes (n = 358), newly diagnosed diabetes by pre-operative HbA1c ≥6.5% (n = 33), and non-qualifying procedures (n = 93). The final cohort included 742 patients: pancreaticoduodenectomy (n = 415), distal pancreatectomy (n = 249), subtotal pancreatectomy (n = 74), and central pancreatectomy (n = 4).

### Figure 1. Figure 1. HbA1c trajectory following pancreatectomy stratified by new-onset diabetes status.
Median HbA1c values (solid lines) with interquartile range (shaded ribbons) are shown at each annual time point for patients who developed new-onset diabetes mellitus (red; n = 154) and those who did not (blue; n = 587). Dashed and dotted reference lines indicate the American Diabetes Association diagnostic thresholds for diabetes mellitus (>=6.5%) and prediabetes (>=5.7%), respectively. Numbers at risk are shown below the figure at each time point. HbA1c = glycated hemoglobin; DM = diabetes mellitus; IQR = interquartile range; Y = year post-pancreatectomy. 

### Figure 2. Estimated HbA1c Trajectories After Pancreatectomy From Linear Mixed‑Effects Models
Estimated marginal mean HbA1c over 10 years is shown by glycemic group using a linear mixed-effects model (Model 2A). The model includes fixed effects for time, glycemic group, and their interaction, adjusted for age, BMI, resection type, neoadjuvant therapy, adenocarcinoma diagnosis, and tobacco use, with a random intercept for patient. Shaded areas represent 95% confidence intervals. The prediabetes group was excluded from visualization due to limited follow-up data. Horizontal lines indicate standard diagnostic thresholds for prediabetes (5.7%) and diabetes (6.5%). The lower panel shows the number of observations at each time point.

### Figure 3. Figure 3. Cumulative incidence of new-onset diabetes mellitus following pancreatectomy accounting for the competing risk of death.
(A) Overall cohort (N = 729); both new-onset DM and death without DM are shown. (B) Stratified by resection type. (C) Stratified by pathologic diagnosis (adenocarcinoma vs other). (D) Stratified by receipt of neoadjuvant therapy. Shaded areas represent 95% confidence intervals. Group differences were assessed using Gray's test. Numbers at risk are shown below each panel at 12-month intervals. Patients with pre-operative HbA1c ≥6.5% were excluded (n = 11). DM = diabetes mellitus; NAT = neoadjuvant therapy; PD = pancreaticoduodenectomy.

### Figure 6. Forest plot of multivariable predictors of new-onset diabetes mellitus following pancreatectomy.
 Odds ratios with 95% confidence intervals from Firth penalized likelihood logistic regression are shown for Model A (full analytic cohort, N = 724) and Model B (pre-operative HbA1c subcohort, N = 206). Red markers (*) denote predictors reaching statistical significance (p < 0.05); orange markers (~) denote trends toward significance (0.05 ≤ p < 0.10). Model A excludes pre-operative HbA1c due to >25% missingness; Model B is restricted to patients with available pre-operative HbA1c. Patients with pre-operative HbA1c ≥6.5% were excluded (n = 11). OR = odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; POPF = postoperative pancreatic fistula; DM = diabetes mellitus; Ref = reference category. 

### Figure 7. Forest plot of multivariable predictors of post-operative exocrine insufficiency following pancreatectomy.
 Odds ratios with 95% confidence intervals from Firth penalized likelihood logistic regression are shown for Model A (full analytic cohort, N = 613) and Model B (pre-operative HbA1c subcohort, N = 164). Red markers (*) denote predictors reaching statistical significance (p < 0.05); orange markers (~) denote trends toward significance (0.05 ≤ p < 0.10). Patients with pre-operative exocrine insufficiency were excluded from the outcome analysis as this precluded attribution of exocrine dysfunction to the surgical procedure. Model A excludes pre-operative HbA1c due to >25% missingness; Model B is restricted to patients with available pre-operative HbA1c. Patients with pre-operative HbA1c ≥6.5% were excluded (n = 11). New-onset diabetes mellitus is included as a concurrent marker of pancreatic parenchymal loss rather than a causal predictor. OR = odds ratio; CI = confidence interval; POPF = postoperative pancreatic fistula; DM = diabetes mellitus; Ref = reference category.
